The system's non-negotiables
🔒
Cooking is locked to 17:00–18:30 on Tuesday + Friday
Never during study hours. Never randomly on a Wednesday because you're hungry and confused. This one rule eliminates the 3-hour food chaos spiral.
🍵
Matcha + meditation is your morning ignition — not optional
Matcha (L-theanine + caffeine) gives calm, sustained focus without jitters or crash. 10-min meditation before it kicks in primes your brain. This combo is worth more than an extra 30 min of study.
📚
NPTEL first principles → slides → problems · always in this order
Never jump into slides without understanding the why. But also never get stuck on first principles for hours. NPTEL gives foundation, slides give exam content, problems give 1.0.
⏱️
Timers are your scaffold, not your willpower
50 min on, 10 off. 20-min nap (alarm set). 20-min break (timer set). Every transition has a timer. You don't rely on "feeling like" transitioning — the timer decides.
🛑
Stuck on a concept? 20-min rule
Set a 20-min timer when stuck. If not solved: write down exactly where you're stuck, move on, come back later or look it up. Don't spend 2 hours on one proof during study block.
📝
Morning planning session decides the whole day
Which subject. Which specific topic. Which meal. Written down. This is the single decision that prevents 10 small decisions from fragmenting your day.
🔋
Sleep 7.5 hrs minimum — non-negotiable
Sleep is when your brain consolidates what you studied. Cutting sleep for study time is a net loss. The students at RWTH getting 1.0 sleep properly. This is not a cliché — it's neuroscience.
🛒
Grocery shopping is Saturday 14:00–17:00 only
2–3 hrs locked in. Never on a weekday. Never impulse. Same list every week. You buy once, you cook twice (Tue + Fri), you eat for 7 days.
🔄
Broken morning ≠ broken day
Missed 07:20 start? Start at 08:00. Slept in until 9? Start at 9. The day isn't cancelled. Just start the next block from wherever you are.
🧠
When locked in, you solve hard problems — trust that
You said it yourself. Your brain can memorise fast and solve hard problems when focused. The system's job is to get you to "locked in" reliably. Once there, trust yourself.
ADHD/AUDHD specific hacks
📍
Same place, same time, same ritual = automatic focus
Matcha → meditation → same desk → same playlist. Your brain learns: this sequence = focus mode. After 2 weeks, you won't need willpower to start. The ritual fires automatically.
✅
Make progress visible · cross things off physically
Paper notebook. Cross off each block. Draw a box, tick it. Visual evidence defeats the ADHD feeling of "I've done nothing today" even when you've done a lot.
🎧
Brown noise or rain sounds for deep blocks
YouTube "brown noise study" or Lofi Girl. Same audio every session = brain cue for focus. Consistent sensory environment reduces ADHD context-switching.
🐜
Overwhelmed? Shrink the task to one sentence
Not "study ICDM thermodynamics." Instead: "open notebook and write the first law of thermodynamics." Do that one thing. Momentum comes from action, not motivation. The next step appears after the first one.
